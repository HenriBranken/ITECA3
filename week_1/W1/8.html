Two Columns: HTML Code vs Rendered Result

Original HTML (displayed)


<h2>&lt;q&gt; (short quote with quotation marks)</h2>
<p>
  Nelson Mandela once said:
  <q>It always seems impossible until it’s done.</q>
</p>

<hr />

<h2>&lt;blockquote&gt; (long quote, indented)</h2>
<blockquote>
  Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the
  world.
</blockquote>

<hr />

<h2>&lt;abbr&gt; (acronym with meaning on hover)</h2>
<p>
  I’m learning <abbr title="HyperText Markup Language">HTML</abbr> and
  <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets">CSS</abbr>. (Hover over the
  abbreviations.)
</p>

<hr />

<h2>&lt;address&gt; (contact information)</h2>
<address>
  Written by: Alex Student<br />
  Email: alex@example.com<br />
  Johannesburg, South Africa
</address>

<hr />

<h2>&lt;cite&gt; (title of a work)</h2>
<p>My favourite book is <cite>Crime and Punishment</cite>.</p>

<hr />

<h2>&lt;bdo&gt; (Bi-Directional Override)</h2>
<p>Normal direction: Hello world</p>
<p>Right-to-left using <code>&lt;bdo dir="rtl"&gt;</code>:</p>
<p><bdo dir="rtl">Hello world</bdo></p>
